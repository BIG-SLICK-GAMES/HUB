21 Hold'em

A blackjack-pressure table game played against other players. Build the strongest total of 21 or less, decide when to stand, and use betting pressure to control the hand.

How to play

Win the pot without going bust

Every player starts with one private hole card. Betting and community-card stages then decide who keeps improving, who locks their score, and who folds under pressure.

Step 1Start with one card

Blinds post, the dealer button rotates, and each player receives one private card.

Step 2Bet or check

If no bet is open, check or open the betting. If a bet is open, call, raise, or fold when eligible.

Step 3Hit or stand

Hit continues to the next community-card step. Stand locks your current total for the rest of the hand.

Step 4Watch the table cards

Action, Stage, Show, and Caboose cards can improve live hands that have not locked.

Step 5Handle pressure

Standing or doubling down stops future cards, but you may still need to call or fold if betting reopens.

Step 6Showdown

All active players reveal. The highest valid total of 21 or less wins, and tied winning totals split the pot.

Player actions

The decisions

21 Hold'em uses familiar betting actions, with blackjack-style score pressure layered on top.

CheckPass the action when no bet is currently open.
CallMatch the current amount required to stay in the hand.
RaiseIncrease the current bet when raising is available.
FoldDrop the hand and forfeit any claim to the pot.
StandLock your current total. Future community cards no longer affect your hand.
Double DownFirst-round option: post the required amount, take a second private card, and lock your total.
If you stand or double down, later betting pressure is resolved with call-or-fold decisions. Raising may also be unavailable when the active action state or all-in state blocks it.
Optional side bets

Extra ways to sweat the hand

Side bets are placed before the hand begins and resolve from your final evaluated cards.

Pays 3:121

Your final evaluated hand total is exactly 21.

Pays 4:1Flush

Your private card and available community cards make three or more cards of one suit.

Pays 5:1Straight

Your private card and available community cards make three or more running ranks.

BonusStraight Flush

A qualifying straight that is also all one suit pays the straight-flush bonus on the Straight side bet.

Official rules

Table rules

SetupBlinds and button

The dealer button rotates every hand. Small blind and big blind are posted by the next two players clockwise.

ScoringAces are flexible

Number cards use face value, face cards count as 10, and aces count as 1 or 11 for the best valid total.

BustOver 21 loses

A total above 21 is bust and cannot win the main pot.

StandingLocked totals

A standing player keeps the score they had when they stood. Later community cards do not help or hurt that hand.

ShowdownAll active cards reveal

Every active player still in the hand must reveal at showdown. Folded players cannot win the pot.

TiesSplit pot

If multiple active players tie for the best valid total, the pot is split between them.

Instant winsFirst-round 21

A first-round blackjack, or a double-down hand that reaches exactly 21, can win immediately under table rules.

Reveal eyeCurrent hand only

The eye icon marks your hole card for reveal this hand, but all active showdown players reveal regardless.

PressureCall or fold

Players who already stood or doubled down can still face later call-or-fold decisions if another player reopens betting.
